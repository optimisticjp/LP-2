# Presenting this prototype to management

This document is a quick guide for showing the group website to the L. P. Savani leadership. It is not part of the website.

---

## The one-line message

> "This is a central digital platform for the entire L. P. Savani Group. Parents can explore every school, understand admissions, see facilities, compare campuses, and contact the right branch from one place. After this group website, we can build full premium websites for each school."

---

## Suggested demo flow (about 5 minutes)

1. **Home page.** Start at the top. Point out the headline, the fast facts (20,000+ students, 500+ faculty, 20+ years, 6 campuses), and scroll to the **campus finder**. Click between campuses to show how a parent picks the right one.
2. **Schools mega menu.** Open the "Schools" menu in the header. Show that every campus is one click away, with location and board, on both desktop and mobile.
3. **A branch page.** Open **Palanpor** (the most complete). Walk through the overview, highlights, facilities, gallery and contact block. Note that each campus can look this polished.
4. **Admissions.** Show the step-by-step journey, the document checklist, the enquiry form (submit it to show the confirmation), and the "Book a campus tour" section.
5. **Infrastructure and gallery.** Show the facilities by category and the filterable gallery.
6. **The proposal page (`/proposal`).** Finish here. This lays out the opportunity, the plan, the phase-wise roadmap and the business benefits.
7. **Mobile.** Open it on a phone. Show the sticky bottom bar (Call, WhatsApp, Visit, Apply) and the mobile menu.

---

## What to emphasise

- **One brand, many campuses.** Parents currently jump between separate sites and pages. This brings everything together with a consistent, premium identity.
- **Admissions made simple.** A clear journey, forms in the right places, and quick actions on mobile, all of which support more enquiries.
- **Real, editable content.** The site already uses the group's real campus details. Content is kept separate from design, so the school can update it easily.
- **A foundation, not a one-off.** This is phase one. It sets up dedicated school websites, an admissions system, and a parent portal later.

---

## How to pitch phase 2

Suggest approving the group website now, and choosing **one campus for a full dedicated website pilot**. That gives the group a strong central platform immediately, and a clear, low-risk way to see what a full individual school site looks like before rolling it out to every campus.

---

## Scope options to discuss

- Group website (this prototype, refined and launched)
- Branch landing pages (included here, can be expanded)
- Full individual school websites (one pilot, then roll out)
- Admissions system integration (enquiry tracking, WhatsApp, dashboards)
- Ongoing maintenance and content updates
